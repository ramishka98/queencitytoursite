Kawarau Suspension Bridge

The Kawarau Suspension Bridge is one of the oldest suspension bridges in New Zealand, built in 1880. It is now a popular spot for bungy jumping and other adrenaline activities. The bridge is located over the Kawarau Gorge, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and rivers.

TSS Earnslaw

The TSS Earnslaw is a vintage steamship that was built in 1912. It is still in operation today and offers visitors a chance to experience the history of New Zealand's lake steamers. The Earnslaw operates cruises on Lake Wakatipu, as well as dining and event services.

Skyline Gondola

The Skyline Gondola takes visitors to the top of Bob's Peak for stunning views of Queenstown and the surrounding area. There is also a restaurant, bar, and luge at the top. The gondola is a popular tourist attraction, and it also offers a great way to get around Queenstown.

Gibbston Valley

Gibbston Valley is a wine region located just a short drive from Queenstown. It is home to some of New Zealand's best wineries, and visitors can take wine tours, visit cellar doors, or simply enjoy a glass of wine at one of the many restaurants. The valley is also a beautiful place to hike or bike.

Arrowtown

Arrowtown is a historic gold mining town located just a short drive from Queenstown. The town is full of charm, with its well-preserved buildings and cobbled streets. Visitors can learn about the town's history at the Arrowtown Museum, or simply wander around the shops and cafes.

Walter Peak

Walter Peak is a high country station located on the other side of Lake Wakatipu from Queenstown. Visitors can take a ferry to the station and enjoy a variety of activities, including sheep shearing, horse trekking, and farm tours. There is also a restaurant and bar at the station, where visitors can enjoy a meal with stunning views of the lake.

Milford Sound

Milford Sound is one of the most popular tourist destinations in New Zealand. This fiord is known for its stunning scenery, with its towering cliffs, waterfalls, and rainforests. Visitors can take boat cruises, kayak, or fly over the fiord. Milford Sound is located about two hours from Queenstown, but it is well worth the trip.

Glenorchy

Glenorchy is a small town located at the head of Lake Wakatipu. It is a popular base for exploring the surrounding area, including the Dart Valley and Routeburn Track. Glenorchy is also a beautiful place to relax and enjoy the scenery.

Cardrona

Cardrona is a historic gold mining town located about 30 minutes from Queenstown. The town is known for its charming streets and shops, as well as its proximity to some of the best ski fields in New Zealand. Cardrona is a great place to visit for a winter getaway or a summer adventure.

Skippers Canyon

Skippers Canyon is a canyon located about an hour from Queenstown. It is a popular spot for jet boating, white water rafting, and bungy jumping. The canyon is also home to some of the most beautiful scenery in New Zealand.
